Exposure times: what exposure times?

This is an experimental view of the exposure times of the Science observations made by Chandra. That is, how long does Chandra spend observing a target in a single observation, rather than a look at how much time is spent on each target. I may well switch to a histogram view, but the cumulative view means I don't have to think about a sensible binning scheme. The data used is for all the data shown in the calendar view, and is split by proposal cycle, as well as there being one curve for all the data (the dotted line).

The Cycle 13 results are different because there are not many observations from this cycle in this data set. It's not so immediately clear to me why the Cycle 17 curve is so different; it is also not complete, but I am not convinced that's the explanation (although the more I look at it, the more I think it might be a significant contributor).
